The Loyalty Tax: Why Your Current Bank Isn't Giving You the Best Deal

April 14, 2026 4 min read KBD Editorial

It’s a harsh reality of the banking world: New customers get better rates than old ones. If you have been with your bank for 3+ years, you are likely paying a "Loyalty Tax"—an interest rate that is 0.5% to 2% higher than what the same bank offers to new applicants.

Why Banks Punish Loyalty

Banks often count on "inertia." They assume you won't go through the hassle of switching lenders. However, a Home Loan Balance Transfer (BT) allows you to switch your outstanding loan to a different lender at a lower rate.

The Power of Switching:

  • The Savings: Even a 0.5% drop on a ₹50L loan can save you ₹5-8 Lakhs over the remaining tenure.
  • Top-up Benefits: Switching often allows you to secure a "Top-up" loan at home loan rates for renovations or other needs.
  • The KBD Process: We analyze your current ROI and match you with lenders who are aggressively poaching high-quality profiles like yours.

Stop being loyal to a bank that isn't loyal to your wallet. If your current ROI is higher than the market standard, it's time to trigger a Balance Transfer. KBD Credit Solutions handles the entire paperwork process so you can start saving from next month.
