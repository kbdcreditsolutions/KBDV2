The Strategic Personal Loan: Why Smart Investors Don't Break their FDs

April 14, 2026 4 min read KBD Editorial

When an emergency strikes or a big-ticket purchase arrives, the instinctive reaction is to liquidate savings—break a Fixed Deposit or sell Mutual Fund units. In 2026, this is often a financial mistake.

The Math of Wealth Preservation

If your Mutual Funds are growing at 15% and your FD is at 7%, breaking them early results in:

  • Lost Compound Interest: You restart your wealth-building clock from zero.
  • Prepayment Penalties: Banks charge you for taking your own money back.
  • Tax Leakage: Capital Gains tax hits you immediately.

A Strategic Personal Loan at 10-11% allows you to keep your 15% growth intact. You aren't just borrowing money; you are protecting your future wealth. Borrowing at 10% to protect 15%+ growth is not debt—it's strategy.
